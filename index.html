The problem is not willpower. The problem is structure. And structure can be changed — starting today.

Tool 01

Pomodoro — Adapted for Salah

Classic Pomodoro is 25 minutes of work, 5 minutes rest. Fine in theory. But nobody explains how it works when you pray five times a day.

The fix: don't squeeze salah into your Pomodoros. Build your Pomodoros around salah.

After Fajr2–3 Pomodoros. Most important task. Brain is clear, world is quiet.
After Dhuhr1–2 Pomodoros. Tasks needing focus — but not peak concentration.
After Asr1 Pomodoro. Admin: messages, planning, lighter work.
After MaghribOne max. Or none. This time is yours.

Practical rule: don't start a Pomodoro if salah is under 20 minutes away. Pray first — then a full unbroken block.

Try it right now. One task. Set a 25-minute timer. Just that.

Tool 02

The "Why Didn't It Work" — 5-Question Breakdown

Most people, when something falls through, do one of two things: move on — or beat themselves up. Neither breaks the pattern. I started doing something different.

After every fallen-through plan — five questions. Not for self-criticism. For diagnostics.

  1. What exactly didn't happen? Not "I was unproductive" — but "I didn't open the project document."
  2. What got in the way? Fatigue? Phone? Task too vague? No time?
  3. Is this a one-time thing, or a pattern I keep repeating?
  4. What will I do differently? Not "try harder." A concrete change: phone in another room, task broken into steps, timer set.
  5. What is my specific action tomorrow morning? One step. Small. Concrete.

Try it tonight. One task you didn't do this week. Five questions. See what you find.

These two tools work. But a system is when all the tools work together — morning structure, a goal written so your brain actually acts on it, 30-day sprints so you're not waiting for January. That's what's in the guide.
